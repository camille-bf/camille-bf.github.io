Interactive Media

For this project, I selected https://alannn.com/index

1/ The first thing I noticed was that the cursor was an animated fly.

2/ During the two minutes, I started to scroll, clicked on the About page, pressed the power button on the landing page, explored the Work page, clicked on the portoflio page interaction in the works page, viewed the projects, clicked on the Links page, and finally checked the Awards button.

3/ I spent the most time navigating between the About and Work pages because I wanted to see both the personal information and the showcased projects. These two pages were the ones that had the most content because its a portfolio page so its what people are the most interested in, skills and works.

4/ In the two minutes experience, the most common actions were scrolling and clicking on everything that looked interactive.

5/ My impression of the website is that it is a portoflio that showcases his skills, not just with the work or technical skills he shares, but rather through interaction and design of the website.

6/ The interactive experience communicates this primary goal just from interacting that he is both a developer and a designer because of how clean layout is, the strong visual identity, and how coherent the design is.

7/ The experience should be explored by clicking through all the pages in his portfolio to fully see his work and technical skills. Users should also take time to notice small interactive details—for example, hovering over the coffee cup triggers a subtle smoke animation. It takes around five minutes, or a little more, to interact with the entire website, which is usually enough to see and appreciate his design and development skills. If he adds new work over time, it’s worth revisiting the site.

8/ The interactive experience communicates how it should be used through subtle signals, such as hover effects. For example, when you hover over the "About" page, the word becomes larger, inviting you to click on it. The animations also help guide the user, and the site is very structured, with a landing page, About page, Work page, Links page, and Awards page, making the navigation clear and intuitive for the users.

9/ the experience references from many different other media forms such as digital art for the illustrations, motion design because of the site’s animations, and of course UI elements that are interactive such as hover effect, scroll, clickable content.

10/ The references communicate that I should be curious, explore everything that looks interactive, and take time to notice details and animations.

11/ The references on the site communicate that I should feel curious, and engaged while interacting with the experience. The use of motion design, hover effects, interactive elements, and a coherent design makes the experience enjoyable and intuitive. The linear organization and structure of the content allows users to explore freely without fear of getting lost, encouraging experimentation and active participation.

12/ The most frustrating part of the interaction is that, while the landing page is well organized and clear, the actual pages like "About" and "Work" could have been better structured. On the "Work" page, only one client project is showcased, while the other two works are his current portfolio and a 3D portfolio he is developing. There are at least two works that he could have logically added to his "Work" page. In the "About" page, his technical skills and awards are shown, but some links are not clickable or don’t lead directly to his work, which can be confusing. Additionally, the awards being listed in multiple places (in the about page and the awards page), makes it harder to fully understand his achievements and shows that the site’s structure could be improved.

13/ The most satisfying part of the interaction is when the user opens the "About" or "Work" page, and the content is presented within an illustration of a computer. This is satisfying because it is an original concept that makes the portfolio feel interactive and immersive, and also reinforces the creativity of the designer’s work instead of a standard portfolio content. And when you click on a project in the "Work" page, you can interact with it as if it was the actual website of that portfolio which is also original and adds to the designer’s attention to detail. I also liked that when you click on the menu button top right, there is a hand animation appears and “pulls down” the menu from the top so that it appears. Moreover, on the landing page for “links” i find it satisfactory that badge notifications appears on the GitHub, LinkedIn and instagram icons when you hover over them. It highlights both the designer’s attention to detail and encourages the user to interact with the interface to find these little details that add to the experience.
